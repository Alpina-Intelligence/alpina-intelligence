# Puck Prophet Purpose

I participate in a couple of NHL hockey pools every year. These are simple pools, not full-fledged fantasy hockey. The tool we have been using is Office Pools. The site works well enough, although I notice it is not too pretty, is slow and laggy, has tons of ads that disrupt the layout of the page, and doesn't render well on mobile. Still, it is a serviceable solution that I've used for years. I am even the admin for one pool and have found managing pools/teams using Office Pools fine, even if their site seems out-of-date compared to modern sports apps like Sleeper.

However, this most recent season there was quite the commotion caused by a change in Office Pool's guest access policy. Previously, a pool could have a name and password set up for anyone to be able to access the pool page to view the teams and standings. This was quite convenient for many people who didn't want to have to sign up for another account just to check out how their team is doing. Signing up gave some benefit but was optional. But for the 2025/26 season this guest access was removed for pools in the basic tier (which had already been increased in price a couple years prior from $20 to $25).
